# PaceBox ad video v2 — pit-lane comedy (the founder rides)

- **Why v2**: the champion-garage scenario proved too complex for the model (two
  characters + elbow-down demo). This one is deliberately simple: one location,
  three beats, no riding footage — the comedy formula that generates reliably.
- **Hero = the founder himself.** ⚠️ **Attach 2–3 clear photos of yourself with
  every generation** (face visible, ideally one in riding gear) — the prompt alone
  will not reproduce you.
- **Grade**: Thai TVC comedy colorimetry (same as the Nivas motosai clips) —
  bright natural daylight, warm skin tones, punchy saturated colors, slight film
  grain. NOT the neon look.
- **The gag**: two superbike riders mock the small Ninja 400 → the hero silently
  shows his phone → their faces drop → deadpan thumbs-up. Lap times never lie.
- **Platform**: Higgsfield · Seedance 2.0 (accepts reference images) · 9:16 · 8 s ·
  single continuous shot · 3–4 takes
- **Golden rules**: no text/logos/UI generated — phone screen angled away, real app
  screenshot composited in post, brand only on the end card.

## Prompt

```
Vertical 9:16, single continuous 8-second shot, fixed camera with a slow subtle
push-in, photorealistic, Thai TV commercial comedy style, bright natural daylight,
warm skin tones, punchy saturated colors, slight film grain.

A sunny pit lane at a race track in Thailand. CHARACTER REFERENCE: the hero is the
same man as in the attached photos. He stands relaxed beside a small green-and-black
sport bike (Ninja 400 class) on a paddock stand, helmet under his arm, a tiny
matte-black telemetry box visible on the bike's tail. Next to him, two riders in
expensive full leathers lean against big 1000cc superbikes, laughing and pointing
mockingly at his small bike. The hero stays completely deadpan, calmly raises his
smartphone toward them (screen angled away from camera). Their laughter dies
instantly — both faces drop into wide-eyed shock, one slowly removes his sunglasses.
The hero turns to camera with a tiny deadpan thumbs-up.

Classic Thai TVC comedy timing: mockery, silent reveal, stunned reaction, deadpan
button. Heat shimmer over the asphalt, pit garages and track in the background. No
text, no logos, no readable brand marks.
```

## Dialogue / VO (post-production)

```
Mocking rider: นินจาสี่ร้อยเนี่ยนะ? 555
(beat — the phone reveal, silence)
Super (burned in at the reveal): เวลาต่อรอบไม่เคยโกหก
```
*Mocking rider: "A Ninja 400, seriously? Hahaha" — Super: "Lap times never lie."*

## Post-production

- At the reveal (t≈5 s): 1 s insert of the real LAP TIMER screenshot (lap 9 ★
  1:20.64, the green delta column) — this is what shuts them up.
- End card: **"PaceBox — รู้ทุกโค้ง เร็วทุกรอบ"** (*"Know every corner, faster every
  lap"*) + logo + QR.
- Sound: pit-lane ambience, laughter cut dead on the phone raise, one dry
  quickshifter pop on the thumbs-up (the PaceBox sonic logo from the jingle).
- Have the Thai lines proofread by a native speaker.
